<p><p>The CO2 laser power supply market features two primary cooling forms: natural air cooling and forced air cooling. Natural air cooling relies on ambient air to dissipate heat, making it simple and energy-efficient, suitable for low to moderate power applications. In contrast, forced air cooling employs fans or blowers to enhance airflow, allowing for better heat management in high-output systems. Each cooling method caters to distinct operational requirements, affecting performance, longevity, and maintenance of CO2 laser systems.</p></p>
