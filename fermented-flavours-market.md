<p><p>The fermented flavors market is primarily segmented into acetic acid bacteria and lactic acid bacteria types. Acetic acid bacteria are primarily involved in the production of vinegar and sour flavors, contributing to the tangy taste in various food products. Lactic acid bacteria, on the other hand, are essential in the fermentation of dairy products, pickles, and sauerkraut, providing mild and creamy flavors while enhancing the texture. Both types are key drivers in the growing demand for artisanal and healthy fermented foods.</p></p>
